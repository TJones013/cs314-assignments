Favorite Healthy Recipes

  • Buffalo Chicken Wrap

    Instructions:

    1. Take chicken breast and slice into even slices.
    2. Heat a non-stick pan to medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook until inside is not longer pink (about 4-5 minutes). Coat finished chicken in hot sauce of choice.
    3. Take a wheat tortilla or flat bread and add a tablespoon of blue cheese. Then top with chicken, some lettuce or spinach, and diced tomatoes.
    4. Serve plain or with celery and/or carrots.

  • Grilled Teryaki Salmon

    Instructions:

    1. Slice salmon into single serving sizes (about 4oz pieces). Marinate these in teryaki sauce for at least 2 hours.
    2. Heat grill to medium heat and let warm before putting on salmon. Cook the salmon on each side until fish is flaky (about 5 minutes per side). If salmon has skin, cook the skin side at high heat for an additional minute to get crispy skin.
    3. Serve with rice and a spinach salad.

  • Turkey Meatloaf

    Instructions:

    1. In a large bowl, mix 2lbs of ground turkey with an egg, about half a cup of bread crumbs, and half a can of condensed tomato soup. Add garlic powder and italian seasoning after mixing.
    2. Preheat convection oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit. When oven is hot, form turkey mixture into a loaf shape inside of a bread tin or other oven safe container. Pour remaining tomato soup over top of meatloaf.
    3. Cook meatloaf for 1 hour or until the center is cooked. Remove from oven and let cool for 10 minutes.
    4. Serve with mashed sweet potato or brown rice.

Lifestyle Tips and Advice

  • One major source of calories that people do not realize is drinks. Most beverages contain at least 100 calories, many with excess amounts of sugar. If you do want to drink things other than water, choose a diet soda or iced tea.

  • Alcohol is another major source of calories and carbohydrates. But if you don't want to be a dab and still want to have a few drinks stick with either light beers and liquors mixed with club soda or diet soda.

  • You can still have fun while trying to live a healthy lifestyle. The occasional cheat day won't kill you, but always consume things in moderation. Do not skip meals and starve yourself. This will cause your body to burn muscle for energy and not fat.
